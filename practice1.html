FUNDAMENTALS

A noun is a word that refers to a thing(book),a person(Betty Croker), an animal(bird), a place(Toronto), a quality(softness), an idea (justice), or an action (yodeling).

EXAMPLES OF A NOUN

Birds are group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong yet lighweight skeleton.

A book is a medium for recording information in the form of writing or images, typically composed of many pages bound together and protected by a cover.

A place is a location. The word is used to describe a specific location , such as the place of shelf, physical environment, a building or locality of special significance, or a particular region or location.

Code tells a computer what actions to take, and writing code is like creating a set of instructions. By learning to write code, you can tell computers what to do or how to behave in a much faster way.

Poem of William SHAKESPEARE

Sonnet 18: Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day? BY WILLIAM SHAKESPEARE

Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day? Thou art more lovely and more temperate: Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May, And summer’s lease hath all too short a date; Sometime too hot the eye of heaven shines, And often is his gold complexion dimm'd; And every fair from fair sometime declines, By chance or nature’s changing course untrimm'd; But thy eternal summer shall not fade, Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow’st; Nor shall death brag thou wander’st in his shade, When in eternal lines to time thou grow’st: So long as men can breathe or eyes can see, So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.
